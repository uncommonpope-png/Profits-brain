# PLT Stack Ranking: The Ultimate Decision Framework for Entrepreneurs

*Published: March 15, 2026*

Every successful entrepreneur has faced this moment: **too many opportunities, not enough time.**

You're presented with three potential projects:
- Project A: High profit potential, boring work, complex tax implications
- Project B: Work you'd love, uncertain revenue, simple structure  
- Project C: Moderate profit, okay work, tax-optimized structure

How do you choose?

Most entrepreneurs use gut feeling, advice from others, or whatever seems "hottest" right now. This leads to scattered focus, half-finished projects, and burnout.

The PLT Framework gives you a systematic approach: **PLT Stack Ranking**.

## The PLT Stack Ranking Method

Step 1: **Score each opportunity** on all three dimensions (1-10)
Step 2: **Weight the dimensions** based on your current situation  
Step 3: **Calculate weighted scores** and rank your options
Step 4: **Apply the decision filters** to validate your choice

Let's work through a real example.

## Case Study: The Consultant's Dilemma

Meet Tom, a marketing consultant with three potential directions:

**Option A: Corporate Training**
- Profit: 9/10 (high-ticket, recurring revenue)
- Love: 4/10 (corporate environment drains him)
- Tax: 6/10 (straightforward but not optimized)

**Option B: Creative Agency**  
- Profit: 6/10 (competitive market, feast/famine cycle)
- Love: 9/10 (creative work energizes him)
- Tax: 4/10 (complex client relationships, unclear structure)

**Option C: Digital Products**
- Profit: 7/10 (scalable but takes time to build)
- Love: 8/10 (enjoys teaching and content creation)
- Tax: 9/10 (clean structure, intellectual property benefits)

## Step 2: Weight Based on Current Situation

Tom's situation:
- **Profit**: High priority (2x weight) - needs consistent income
- **Love**: Medium priority (1x weight) - burned out from past work
- **Tax**: High priority (2x weight) - wants clean, sustainable business

Weighted scores:
- **Option A**: (9×2) + (4×1) + (6×2) = 34 points
- **Option B**: (6×2) + (9×1) + (4×2) = 29 points  
- **Option C**: (7×2) + (8×1) + (9×2) = 40 points

**Winner: Option C (Digital Products)**

## Step 4: Apply Decision Filters

Before committing, run through the PLT decision filters:

### The Profit Filter
- Is this sustainable long-term? ✓ (Digital products scale)
- Can I price this properly? ✓ (Clear value proposition)
- Will this create real value for customers? ✓ (Solves real problems)

### The Love Filter  
- Am I energized by this work? ✓ (Teaching and content creation)
- Does this play to my strengths? ✓ (Marketing expertise + communication skills)
- Will I still enjoy this in 2 years? ✓ (Continuous learning and growth)

### The Tax Filter
- Is the structure clean and scalable? ✓ (Digital products, clear IP ownership)
- Are the risks manageable? ✓ (Lower liability than consulting)
- Can I optimize for efficiency? ✓ (Automated delivery, global reach)

All filters pass. Option C it is.

## Common PLT Stack Ranking Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Equal weighting**  
Not all dimensions matter equally in every situation. A cash-strapped startup should weight Profit higher. A burned-out executive should weight Love higher.

**Mistake 2: Ignoring your weaknesses**
Scoring high on Love but low on Tax? You need either education or partnership before proceeding.

**Mistake 3: Static thinking**
Your weights change over time. Reassess quarterly.

**Mistake 4: Perfect scores**
If everything scores 9-10, you're not being honest. Every opportunity has tradeoffs.

## Advanced PLT: Time Horizon Weighting

Modify your weighting based on time horizon:

**0-6 months**: Weight Profit heavily (you need cash flow)
**6-18 months**: Balance all three dimensions  
**18+ months**: Weight Love and Tax more heavily (sustainability matters)
