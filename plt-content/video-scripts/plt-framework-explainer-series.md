## Video 2: "PLT Stack Ranking: How to Choose Between Good Opportunities"

### TUTORIAL SECTION (15 seconds - 7 minutes)

**[Screen share: Spreadsheet template]**

**CRAIG**: This is PLT Stack Ranking. Four steps, every time:

**Step One: Score each opportunity**

**[Pointing to spreadsheet]**

Rate every option, one to ten, on all three dimensions. Let's use a real example.

Tom, marketing consultant, had three paths:

**[Filling out spreadsheet live]**

Corporate Training:
- Profit: 9 (high-ticket, recurring)
- Love: 4 (corporate environment drains him)
- Tax: 6 (straightforward but not optimized)

Creative Agency:
- Profit: 6 (competitive, feast and famine)
- Love: 9 (creative work energizes him)
- Tax: 4 (complex client relationships)

Digital Products:
- Profit: 7 (scalable but takes time)
- Love: 8 (enjoys teaching and content)
- Tax: 9 (clean structure, IP benefits)

**Step Two: Weight based on your situation**

**[Adjusting spreadsheet]**

Tom needed consistent income, so:
- Profit: 2x weight (needs cash flow)
- Love: 1x weight  
- Tax: 2x weight (wants clean business)

**Step Three: Calculate weighted scores**

**[Showing calculations]**

Corporate: (9×2) + (4×1) + (6×2) = 34
Agency: (6×2) + (9×1) + (4×2) = 29  
Digital: (7×2) + (8×1) + (9×2) = 40

Winner: Digital products.

**Step Four: Apply decision filters**

**[New screen: Decision filter checklist]**

But scores are just step one. Now the filters:

- Will he still enjoy this in two years? Yes
- Can he scale without burning out? Yes
- Is the structure sustainable long-term? Yes

All filters pass. Tom went digital.

**[Results slide]**

Eighteen months later: consistent six-figure income, loves the work, clean tax structure.

**[Animation showing timeline]**

Your priorities should shift:

0-6 months: Weight Profit heavily (survival)
6-18 months: Balance all three
18+ months: Weight Love and Tax more (sustainability)

**[Back to Tom's example]**

Tom was month four of building his business. Perfect time to weight Profit higher.

If he was month twenty, he might weight Love and Tax more heavily.

**[On screen: Craig's actual scores]**

**CRAIG**: Here's what I did to fix it. And you can apply this exact process to your business.

**[Animation showing task analysis]**

- Energizes me (+1)
- Neutral (0)  
- Drains me (-1)

70% of my tasks were drains or neutrals. That had to change.

- Print logistics (moved to print-on-demand)

- Social media management (delegated to specialist)
- Basic editing (hired freelance editor)

**[Business structure diagram]**

I simplified the entire operation:
- Consolidated to one main LLC
- Automated payments and royalties  
- Implemented proper expense tracking
- Set up quarterly tax planning

**Phase Three: Focus on what energizes me**  

**[New focus areas]**

This freed me to focus on:
- Writing (what I love most)
- Teaching frameworks (my strength)
- Strategic partnerships (high-impact, energizing)
- Course development (scalable and fulfilling)

## Video 5: "PLT for Beginners: Start Here"
**Length**: 12-15 minutes  
**Format**: Complete beginner's guide with step-by-step implementation

---

### HOOK (0-15 seconds)

**CRAIG**: If you've heard about the PLT Framework but don't know where to start, this video is for you.

### FOUNDATION (15 seconds - 3 minutes)

**CRAIG**: PLT stands for Profit, Love, Tax. But it's not what you think.

**[Breaking down each element]**

**Profit** isn't just money. It's sustainable value creation - the engine that keeps everything running.

**Love** isn't just passion. It's alignment - work that energizes rather than drains you.

**Tax** isn't just accounting. It's structure - the legal and financial frameworks that make everything possible.

**[Problem graphic]**

Most business advice tells you to optimize for one:
- "Follow your passion" (Love only)
- "Maximize profits" (Profit only)  
- "Get your paperwork right" (Tax only)

**[Solution graphic]**

PLT forces you to consider all three, always.

**[Real example]**

Think of it like a three-legged stool. Remove any leg and the whole thing collapses.

### THE ASSESSMENT (3-6 minutes)

**CRAIG**: Step one is always assessment. Let's do this together.

**[On screen: Assessment framework]**

Rate your current situation, one to ten:

**Profit Assessment:**
- Are you generating sustainable, growing revenue?
- Can you predict your income three months out?
- Do customers pay premium prices for your work?
- Are you building something that could run without you?

**[Pause for reflection]**

Be honest. Write down your number.

**Love Assessment:**  
- Do you wake up excited about your work?
- Does your work energize or drain you?
- Are you using your natural strengths?
- Would you do this work even if you were financially secure?

**[Another pause]**

Write that number down too.

**Tax Assessment:**
- Do you have proper business structure?
- Are you optimizing for tax efficiency legally?  
- Do you understand your financial obligations?
- Is your business legally protected from personal liability?

**[Final pause]**

Last number.

**[Results interpretation]**

Now look at your three numbers:
- All above 7? You're in great shape, focus on optimization
- One or two below 7? Those are your priority areas  
- Any below 5? Crisis mode - address immediately

### IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP (6-10 minutes)

**CRAIG**: Based on your scores, here's your roadmap:

**[Roadmap graphic]**

**If Profit was your lowest score:**

**[Profit improvement section]**

Priority one: Revenue sustainability
- Audit your current revenue streams
- Identify which customers pay the most for the least effort  
- Raise prices on new clients by 20%
- Create systems to predict and manage cash flow

Quick wins this week:
- Track all business metrics in a simple spreadsheet
- Interview your best clients about why they chose you
- Calculate your true hourly rate including all business tasks

**If Love was your lowest score:**

**[Love improvement section]**

Priority one: Energy alignment
- List all your weekly tasks
- Rate each: energizing (+1), neutral (0), draining (-1)
- Eliminate or delegate everything that drains you
- Double down on what energizes you

Quick wins this week:  
- Say no to one project that doesn't excite you
- Spend two hours on work you absolutely love
- Identify your top three natural strengths

**If Tax was your lowest score:**

**[Tax improvement section]**

Priority one: Structure and protection
- Get proper business entity (LLC or Corp)  
- Open business bank account  
- Implement expense tracking system
- Understand your tax obligations and deadlines

Quick wins this week:
- Download QuickBooks Simple Start or similar
- Schedule consultation with business accountant  
- Research business structure options for your situation

### ADVANCED CONCEPTS (10-13 minutes)

**CRAIG**: Once you've addressed your lowest score, here are the advanced concepts:

**[PLT Decision Filter graphic]**

**The PLT Decision Filter:**

Before any major business decision, ask:
1. Does this create sustainable value? (Profit)
2. Will this energize or drain me? (Love)  
3. What's the optimal structure for this? (Tax)

If any answer is "no" or "I don't know," you're not ready.

**[Stack Ranking preview]**

**PLT Stack Ranking:**

When choosing between opportunities:
- Score each option on all three dimensions
- Weight based on your current priorities
- Calculate weighted totals
- Choose the highest score that passes decision filter

**[Time Horizon Weighting]**

**Time Horizon Weighting:**

Your PLT priorities should shift:
- 0-6 months: Weight Profit higher (need cash flow)
- 6-18 months: Balance all three  
- 18+ months: Weight Love and Tax higher (sustainability)

### COMMON MISTAKES (13-14 minutes)

**CRAIG**: Mistakes I see beginners make:

**[Mistake 1 graphic]**

**Mistake 1**: Optimizing pillars separately
- Don't have "Profit months" and "Love months"
- Every decision should consider all three

**Mistake 2**: Perfect scores everywhere  
- If everything is a 9-10, you're not being honest
- Every business has tradeoffs

**Mistake 3**: Static thinking
- Your PLT priorities change over time
- Reassess quarterly

**Mistake 4**: Ignoring implementation
- Reading about PLT isn't the same as using PLT
- Start with small decisions and build the habit
