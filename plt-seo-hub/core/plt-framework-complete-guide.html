The PLT Implementation Process

1

Business Opportunity Assessment

Before applying PLT filters, clearly define your business opportunity. This could be a new venture, product launch, partnership, or strategic pivot.

Deliverable: One-page opportunity description including target market, value proposition, and initial assumptions.

2

Profit Pillar Analysis

Evaluate the financial sustainability and growth potential:

  • Revenue Model: How will you make money?
  • Market Size: Is the addressable market large enough?
  • Unit Economics: Do the numbers work at scale?
  • Competitive Advantage: What prevents easy replication?
  • Scalability: Can you grow without proportional cost increases?

Score: Rate 1-10 based on profit potential and sustainability.

3

Love Pillar Evaluation

Assess personal and organizational alignment:

  • Passion Fit: Does this energize you or drain you?
  • Values Alignment: Does it match your core beliefs?
  • Skill Match: Do you have or can you develop necessary competencies?
  • Long-term Interest: Will you still care in 5 years?
  • Team Resonance: Can you attract people who share the vision?

Score: Rate 1-10 based on authentic passion and alignment.

4

Tax Pillar Impact Assessment

Determine positive societal contribution:

  • Problem Solving: What real problem does this solve?
  • Stakeholder Benefit: Who wins besides you?
  • Environmental Impact: Net positive or negative for the planet?
  • Community Effect: Does this strengthen or weaken communities?
  • Knowledge Sharing: Do you give back expertise and insights?

Score: Rate 1-10 based on genuine positive impact.

5

PLT Integration & Decision

Combine all three scores and apply decision criteria:

  • Total Score: Add all three pillar scores (max 30)
  • Minimum Thresholds: Each pillar must score ≥6 (no pillar can be ignored)
  • Sweet Spot: Look for opportunities scoring 24+ total with balanced pillars
  • Red Flags: Avoid anything scoring <5 on any single pillar

Advanced PLT Strategies

1. PLT Optimization

Once you identify a PLT opportunity, optimize each pillar:

  • Profit Enhancement: Find ways to increase margins, reduce churn, expand market
  • Love Amplification: Delegate tasks you don't enjoy, focus on what energizes you
  • Tax Multiplication: Build giving back into the business model, not just profits

2. PLT Portfolio Approach

Instead of one perfect PLT business, build a portfolio:

  • Cash Cow: High profit, medium love/tax - funds other ventures
  • Passion Project: High love, medium profit/tax - personal fulfillment
  • Impact Venture: High tax, medium profit/love - legacy building

3. PLT Evolution

PLT scores can change over time:

  • Market shifts can affect profit potential
  • Personal growth can change what you love
  • Social awareness can reveal new tax opportunities

Re-evaluate quarterly and adjust strategy accordingly.

⚠️ Common PLT Pitfalls

  • Profit Obsession: Using PLT to justify profit-first thinking
  • Love Delusion: Confusing what you think you should love with what you actually love
  • Tax Theater: Adding superficial "good deeds" without real impact
  • Analysis Paralysis: Over-analyzing instead of testing and iterating

✅ Signs Your PLT Implementation is Working

  • You're excited to work on your business, not just the results
  • Revenue grows sustainably without ethical compromises
  • Customers, employees, and partners genuinely believe in your mission
  • You can clearly articulate how your success benefits others
  • Long-term thinking comes naturally because all pillars are aligned
