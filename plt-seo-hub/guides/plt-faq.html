🎯 PLT Basics
What does PLT stand for in business? ▼

PLT stands for Profit Love Tax - a business framework created by Craig Jones that evaluates opportunities across three essential pillars:

  • Profit: Sustainable financial success and growth
  • Love: Personal passion, values alignment, and fulfillment
  • Tax: Social responsibility and positive impact on society

The framework ensures businesses succeed financially while staying true to the founder's values and contributing positively to the world.

What is the Tax pillar in PLT? ▼

The Tax pillar represents your responsibility to give back to society and the systems that enabled your success. It's not about literal taxes, but about:

  • Solving real problems that matter to people
  • Creating positive environmental impact
  • Strengthening communities and relationships
  • Sharing knowledge and expertise
  • Contributing to the greater good beyond just profit

Think of it as your "social dividend" - what you owe back to the world for your success.

Who created the PLT framework? ▼

Craig Jones created the PLT framework based on his experience as an entrepreneur and author. After building multiple businesses and observing common patterns of success and failure, Craig developed PLT to address the fundamental challenge of building businesses that are:

  • Financially sustainable (not just profitable in the short term)
  • Personally fulfilling (aligned with values and passion)
  • Socially beneficial (contributing positively to the world)

Craig has documented PLT principles in his books and through PLT Press, making the framework accessible to entrepreneurs worldwide.

Is PLT just another business buzzword? ▼

No. PLT is a practical decision-making framework with specific tools and measurable outcomes. Unlike vague concepts, PLT provides:

  • Concrete scoring systems (rate each pillar 1-10)
  • Clear decision criteria (pursue opportunities scoring 20+ total with no pillar below 6)
  • Practical tools (PLT Canvas, Calculator, Assessment templates)
  • Real case studies and examples
  • Measurable results across all three pillars

PLT is designed to be actionable, not aspirational.

🛠 Implementation
How do you implement the PLT framework? ▼

Follow these steps to implement PLT in your business:

  1. Assessment: Use the PLT Calculator to score your current business or opportunity
  2. Decision Making: Apply PLT filters to every major business decision
  3. Goal Setting: Create objectives that advance all three pillars
  4. Team Building: Hire people who align with your complete PLT mission
  5. Measurement: Track financial, satisfaction, and impact metrics equally
  6. Regular Reviews: Monthly PLT alignment check-ins

Start with the PLT Canvas worksheet to map out your specific profit, love, and tax definitions.

What's a good PLT score for a business opportunity? ▼

PLT scoring guidelines:

  • 25-30: Exceptional opportunity (top 5%) - pursue immediately
  • 20-24: Strong opportunity - likely worth pursuing
  • 15-19: Decent opportunity but needs improvement in weak areas
  • 10-14: Significant concerns - major changes needed
  • Below 10: Pass or completely reimagine the opportunity
Critical rule: No single pillar should score below 6, regardless of total score. A 9-9-2 (total 20) is worse than a 7-7-7 (total 21) because the Tax pillar is critically weak.
How often should you review PLT alignment? ▼

PLT review schedule:

  • Daily: Quick PLT filter for major decisions
  • Weekly: Review progress on all three pillars
  • Monthly: Deep assessment using PLT Calculator
  • Quarterly: Strategic realignment and goal setting
  • Annually: Complete framework review and evolution

The key is making PLT a living framework, not a one-time assessment. Market conditions, personal growth, and social needs all evolve over time.

Can you apply PLT to an existing business? ▼

Absolutely. Many entrepreneurs use PLT to audit and improve existing businesses:

  1. Current State Assessment: Score your existing business honestly
  2. Identify Gaps: Which pillar needs the most improvement?
  3. Create Improvement Plan: Specific actions to strengthen weak pillars
  4. Gradual Implementation: Avoid dramatic changes that disrupt operations
  5. Team Communication: Help employees understand the PLT evolution
Example: A successful consulting firm scored high on Profit (8/10) and Love (7/10) but low on Tax (3/10). They added pro bono work for nonprofits and open-sourced their methodology, raising their Tax score to 7/10 while actually improving client attraction and employee satisfaction.
💼 Business Applications
Does PLT work for all types of businesses? ▼

PLT is framework-agnostic and works for:

  • Tech startups: SaaS, apps, platforms
  • Service businesses: Consulting, agencies, freelancing
  • Physical products: E-commerce, manufacturing, retail
  • Creative ventures: Content creation, art, entertainment
  • Investment decisions: Stocks, real estate, partnerships
  • Career choices: Job opportunities, career pivots

The specific implementation varies, but the three-pillar evaluation works universally. A restaurant and a software company will have different profit models and tax contributions, but both benefit from PLT alignment.

How does PLT help with pricing decisions? ▼

PLT provides a holistic approach to pricing:

  • Profit Lens: Ensure pricing supports sustainable business model and growth
  • Love Lens: Price in a way that feels authentic and aligns with your values
  • Tax Lens: Consider pricing impact on accessibility and social benefit
Example: A business coach could charge $5,000/month (high profit) but realized this excluded many entrepreneurs who needed help. They created a tiered model: premium 1:1 coaching ($3,000), group coaching ($500), and free resources, satisfying all three PLT pillars.
Can PLT help with hiring decisions? ▼

Yes! Apply PLT to evaluate potential team members:

  • Profit Impact: Will this person contribute to sustainable business growth?
  • Love Alignment: Do they share your values and mission?
  • Tax Contribution: Do they enhance your positive impact capability?

Candidates who score well on all three pillars become cultural multipliers, while those who only fit one dimension often create friction or underperform long-term.

How does PLT affect investment and funding decisions? ▼

PLT helps evaluate both raising and providing investment:

Raising capital:

  • Seek investors who support your complete PLT mission
  • Avoid funding that would compromise your Love or Tax pillars
  • Use PLT story to attract values-aligned investors

Investment decisions:

  • Evaluate opportunities beyond just financial returns
  • Consider personal interest and social impact
  • Build portfolio aligned with your complete value system
⚖️ Framework Comparisons
How is PLT different from Lean Startup methodology? ▼

PLT and Lean Startup serve different purposes:

  • PLT: Strategic direction - which opportunities to pursue
  • Lean Startup: Tactical execution - how to build efficiently

Key differences:

  • Focus: PLT emphasizes holistic success; Lean focuses on product-market fit
  • Values: PLT integrates values from day one; Lean is values-neutral
  • Sustainability: PLT optimizes for long-term endurance; Lean optimizes for speed

Many entrepreneurs successfully combine both: use PLT for strategy, Lean for execution.

Is PLT similar to triple bottom line (People, Planet, Profit)? ▼

PLT and Triple Bottom Line share some similarities but have key differences:

Similarities: Both consider profit alongside social/environmental impact

Key Differences:

  • Personal Dimension: PLT includes founder passion/values (Love pillar)
  • Decision Framework: PLT provides specific scoring and decision criteria
  • Individual Focus: PLT works for individual entrepreneurs, not just corporations
  • Practical Tools: PLT offers calculators, templates, and implementation guides

PLT is more personal and actionable than traditional Triple Bottom Line accounting.

How does PLT relate to ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance)? ▼

PLT and ESG complement each other:

  • ESG: External measurement framework for investors
  • PLT: Internal decision-making framework for entrepreneurs

PLT's Tax pillar often leads to strong ESG performance, but PLT goes beyond ESG by including:

  • Personal fulfillment and values alignment
  • Practical decision-making tools
  • Founder-centric perspective
  • Holistic business success definition
❓ Common Concerns
Won't focusing on values and impact reduce profits? ▼

Actually, the opposite is often true. Research shows purpose-driven businesses often outperform purely profit-focused ones:

  • Premium pricing: Customers pay more for brands they believe in
  • Lower marketing costs: Authentic mission drives word-of-mouth
  • Employee retention: Values-aligned employees stay longer and perform better
  • Customer loyalty: Purpose creates emotional connection beyond price
  • Future-proofing: Aligned with increasing consumer and regulatory expectations

The key is finding opportunities where all three pillars reinforce each other, not compete.

What if I can't find opportunities that score high on all three pillars? ▼

This is common and indicates you need to either:

  1. Get Creative: Look for innovative ways to align all three pillars
  2. Modify the Opportunity: Adjust your approach to strengthen weak pillars
  3. Explore Different Opportunities: Keep searching for better-aligned options
  4. Portfolio Approach: Balance multiple ventures that excel in different areas
Example: If you love coding but struggling to find profitable + socially beneficial opportunities, consider: open source tools for nonprofits, educational platforms for underserved communities, or efficiency software for small businesses.
Is PLT too idealistic for the real business world? ▼

No. PLT is based on successful real-world businesses:

  • Patagonia: Billion-dollar revenue with environmental activism
  • Warby Parker: Disrupted eyewear with buy-one-give-one model
  • Buffer: $20M+ ARR with radical transparency and values
  • LocalTable: (Case study) $50K/month helping independent restaurants

PLT doesn't require perfection - it requires intention. Even incremental improvements in Love and Tax pillars often strengthen the Profit pillar.

How do you measure success with PLT? ▼

Track metrics across all three pillars:

Profit Metrics:

  • Revenue growth, profit margins, customer acquisition cost
  • Customer lifetime value, retention rate
  • Financial sustainability indicators

Love Metrics:

  • Personal energy and motivation levels
  • Time spent on activities you enjoy
  • Employee satisfaction and retention
  • Values alignment in decision-making

Tax Metrics:

  • Social impact measurement (people helped, problems solved)
  • Environmental footprint
  • Community contribution
  • Knowledge sharing and giving back
🚀 Advanced Topics
Can PLT scores change over time? ▼

Absolutely. PLT scores evolve due to:

  • Market Changes: Competition, regulation, or economic shifts affect profit potential
  • Personal Growth: Your interests, values, and skills develop over time
  • Social Needs: New problems emerge or existing problems become more/less critical
  • Business Evolution: Your company's capabilities and impact change

Regular PLT reviews help you adapt strategy as conditions change. What scores 8/10 today might score 6/10 next year, and vice versa.

How does PLT work for teams and partnerships? ▼

PLT can be applied to group decision-making:

  1. Individual Assessment: Each person scores the opportunity independently
  2. Discussion: Share and discuss different perspectives on each pillar
  3. Consensus Building: Find areas of agreement and address concerns
  4. Role Alignment: Ensure each person's role aligns with their strongest PLT pillars

Teams with aligned PLT values make faster decisions and experience less conflict over strategic direction.

What are the most common PLT implementation mistakes? ▼

Avoid these PLT pitfalls:

  • Profit-First Trap: Using PLT to justify profit-first thinking with superficial Love/Tax elements
  • Love Delusion: Confusing what you think you should love with what you actually love
  • Tax Theater: Adding token "good deeds" without genuine impact integration
  • Analysis Paralysis: Over-analyzing instead of testing and learning
  • Static Thinking: Not adapting PLT scores as conditions change
  • Solo Decision Making: Not involving key stakeholders in PLT assessment
